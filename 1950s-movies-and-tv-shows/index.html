1950s Movies and TV Shows

A list of notable movies and TV shows from the 1950s, highlighting popular titles that shaped the entertainment landscape of the decade.

20,000 Leagues Under the Sea

A 1954 adventure film about Captain Nemo and the mysterious submarine Nautilus exploring the ocean depths.

Adventures of Superman

A TV series (1952-1958) featuring Superman fighting crime with the help of his friends at the Daily Planet.

Around the World in 80 Days

A 1956 film adaptation of Jules Verne's classic about a race against time to circumnavigate the globe.

Ben Hur

An epic 1959 historical drama centered on a Jewish prince betrayed by a Roman friend, featuring iconic chariot race scenes.

Cheyenne

A western TV series (1955-1963) following the adventures of Cheyenne Bodie, a cowboy roaming the American frontier.

Cinderella

The 1950 Disney animated classic retelling the timeless fairy tale of the kind-hearted Cinderella.

Father Knows Best

A sitcom (1954-1960) portraying a wholesome family life led by a wise father and his supportive wife.

From Here to Eternity

A 1953 drama about army life just before the Pearl Harbor attack, exploring complex relationships and conflicts.

Giant

A 1956 drama starring Elizabeth Taylor and Rock Hudson centered on family dynamics and the Texas oil industry.

Gunsmoke

A long-running western TV series (1955-1975) following Marshal Matt Dillon's efforts to maintain law and order.

I Love Lucy

A beloved sitcom (1951-1957) starring Lucille Ball, focusing on the comedic adventures of Lucy Ricardo.

Lady and the Tramp

A 1955 animated Disney film telling the romantic tale of a refined Cocker Spaniel and a streetwise mutt.

Lassie

TV series (1954-1973) about a heroic Rough Collie dog and her human family audiences adored.

Leave It to Beaver

A TV sitcom (1957-1963) chronicling the innocence and adventures of a suburban boy and his family.

Maverick

A western TV series (1957-1962) featuring a charming gambler navigating the Old West with wit and cynicism.

Our Miss Brooks

A sitcom (1952-1956) about an outspoken high school teacher and her humorous interactions with students and colleagues.

Peter Pan

The 1953 animated Disney film depicting the adventures of the boy who never grows up in Neverland.

Perry Mason

A legal drama TV series (1957-1966) following the brilliant defense attorney solving complex cases.

The Adventures of Ozzie & Harriet

A TV sitcom (1952–1966) portraying the real-life family life of the Nelsons with gentle humor and warmth.

The Bridge on the River Kwai

A 1957 World War II epic focusing on British POWs forced to build a strategic bridge for the Japanese.

The Donna Reed Show

A family sitcom (1958-1966) centered on a loving mother and housewife managing home and community life.

The George Burns and Gracie Allen Show

A TV comedy series (1950-1958) featuring the famous duo’s playful and zany routines.

The Honeymooners

A short-lived but iconic sitcom (1955-1956) about blue-collar workers and their comical domestic life.

The Lone Ranger

A western TV series (1949-1957) featuring a masked Texas Ranger and his Native American partner fighting crime.

The Roy Rogers Show

A western TV series (1951-1957) starring Roy Rogers, "The King of the Cowboys," and his famous horse Trigger.

White Christmas

A 1954 musical film about a duo of entertainers planning a Christmas show to save a Vermont inn.

Sleeping Beauty

The 1959 Disney animated fairy tale about Princess Aurora and the curse cast by Maleficent.
