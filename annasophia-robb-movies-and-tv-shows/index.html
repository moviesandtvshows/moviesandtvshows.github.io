Biographical Overview of AnnaSophia Robb

AnnaSophia Robb (born December 8, 1993, in Denver, Colorado) is an American actress, singer, and model who began her career as a child actress. She gained early fame starring in adaptations of popular novels, such as "Because of Winn-Dixie" and Tim Burton's "Charlie and the Chocolate Factory." Known for her versatile performances, Robb went on to star in "Bridge to Terabithia," "Soul Surfer," and the TV series "The Carrie Diaries," portraying a young Carrie Bradshaw. With a background in modeling and music, Robb continues to expand her career in film and television with acclaimed roles.
