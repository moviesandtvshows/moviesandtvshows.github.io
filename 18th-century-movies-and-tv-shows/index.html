18th Century Movies and TV Shows: Historical Dramas and Period Pieces

Exploring 18th century movies and tv shows offers a window into the vibrant history, lavish costumes, and dramatic stories of the 1700s. From aristocratic intrigues to revolutionary tales, these films and series immerse viewers in one of the most fascinating centuries.

The Duchess (2008)

Chronicle of the life of Georgiana, Duchess of Devonshire, an 18th-century aristocrat known for her active political involvement, extravagant lifestyle, and complex personal relationships. The film, starring Keira Knightley, is notable for its refined historical setting and emotional drama.

 

Movies

Admiral Ushakov

A historical drama about the legendary Russian naval commander during the 18th century. The film highlights his major battles and contributions to Russia's naval supremacy.

Amadeus

A richly dramatic portrayal of the life of Wolfgang Amadeus Mozart, exploring his genius and the intense rivalry with Antonio Salieri. The film balances historical facts with artistic interpretation.

Barry Lyndon

Stanley Kubrick’s visually stunning adaptation of William Makepeace Thackeray's novel about a charismatic but flawed Irish adventurer in 18th-century Europe. The film is acclaimed for its period detail and cinematography.

Casanova (2005)

A romantic drama depicting the famous 18th-century Venetian lover Giacomo Casanova. The film captures his escapades, wit, and charm amidst lavish settings.

Fanfan la Tulipe

An adventurous and swashbuckling comedy about a young soldier in 18th-century France who becomes a hero during the reign of King Louis XV. Known for its lively action and humor.

Felicity: An American Girl Adventure

A family-friendly film set during the American Revolution focusing on a young girl’s experiences in 18th-century Williamsburg, highlighting themes of loyalty and courage.

 

The Last of the Mohicans

Set during the French and Indian War, this epic romance and adventure film tells the story of a frontier scout caught between warring factions and his love for a British officer’s daughter.

Outlander

A blend of historical drama and fantasy where a woman from the 20th century is transported back to 18th-century Scotland during the Jacobite uprising. It features action and romance.

Quills

A provocative drama about the life of the Marquis de Sade, focusing on his controversial writings and imprisonment during the period of the French Revolution.

Valmont

An adaptation of the classic novel "Les Liaisons Dangereuses," revolving around the romantic intrigues and manipulations among French aristocrats in the 18th century.

The King's Whore

This drama chronicles the life of a countess caught in a web of political and romantic conflicts at a royal court in 18th-century Italy.

Yellowbeard

A comedic pirate adventure about a fearsome 18th-century pirate searching for buried treasure, filled with slapstick humor and parodies of pirate lore.

Fellini's Casanova (1976)

An avant-garde interpretation of Casanova’s life by Federico Fellini, blending fantasy and reality to explore themes of desire and loneliness.

The Nun (2013)

A period drama set in 18th-century France about a young woman forced to become a nun amidst religious and political turmoil.

The Amorous Adventures of Moll Flanders

A film adaptation of Daniel Defoe’s novel about the life and loves of a clever woman navigating the social conditions of the 18th century.

The Bohemian Girl (1936)

A romantic drama involving gypsies and nobility set in 18th-century Europe, touching upon themes of identity and social divides.

The Barber of Seville

Multiple adaptations of the classic 18th-century comic opera focusing on the clever barber Figaro and his schemes in romantic pursuits.

The Black Cavalier

A swashbuckling film about a masked hero fighting injustice in 18th-century France, mixing adventure and romance.

The Bawdy Adventures of Tom Jones

A comedic retelling of the picaresque novel following the exploits of the lovable rogue Tom Jones in 18th-century England.

The Blood on Satan's Claw

A horror film set in late 17th and early 18th-century England about witchcraft and dark forces threatening a rural community.

Brave New Land

A period drama exploring colonial expansion and indigenous relations in the 18th century.

Becoming Jane

A fictionalized biography of Jane Austen’s early life and her romantic relationships, set at the turn of the 18th century.

The Ambassador (1936)

A political drama centered on court intrigues and diplomatic tensions during the 18th century.

TV Series

Blackadder the Third

A historical comedy series set in the late 18th and early 19th centuries following Edmund Blackadder and his misadventures during the Regency era.

The Cook of Castamar

A Spanish period drama about love and secrets in 18th-century Madrid, centered on a nobleman and his personal cook.

The First Churchills

A BBC historical drama depicting the lives of John and Sarah Churchill during the late 17th and early 18th centuries, focusing on political power and personal dynamics.

Peshwa Bajirao

An Indian historical drama series chronicling the life of Bajirao I, a prominent Maratha Peshwa from the 18th century, showcasing his military campaigns and personal life.

Exploring the Best 18th Century Movies and TV Shows: A Personal Journey into History and Entertainment

Have you ever wondered what it was truly like to live in the 18th century, or wished to immerse yourself in stories that capture the drama, grandeur, and complexity of that fascinating era? I certainly have.

As someone deeply passionate about history and cinematic storytelling, I embarked on an exploration to find the finest movies and TV shows set in the 1700s — a period bustling with revolutions, royal intrigues, artistic brilliance, and unforgettable tales.

This article will guide you through carefully curated works that bring the 18th century to life, revealing not only the historical context but the emotional resonance these films and series offer.

Why Do 18th Century Movies and TV Shows Captivate Us?

The 18th century was a transformative period marked by seismic shifts like the American and French Revolutions, the Enlightenment, and cultural revolutions in art, philosophy, and politics.

Movies and TV shows from or about this era transport viewers into lavishly detailed worlds filled with powdered wigs, ornate palaces, and profound conflicts.

For me, the thrill is how these productions weave grand historical events with intimate human experiences—love, betrayal, ambition, and survival.

They satisfy both an appetite for informative history and rich entertainment. When people search for "18th century movies and tv shows," they are often seeking engaging stories that educate and entertain simultaneously.

Must-Watch 18th Century TV Series and Miniseries

  • Catherine the Great (2015): A richly detailed miniseries about one of Russia's most influential rulers.
  • Black Sails: A gritty pirate drama that brings 18th-century maritime culture vividly to life.
  • The Great (2020–): A satirical yet compelling take on Catherine the Great's ascent and court intrigues.
  • John Adams (2008): An acclaimed miniseries chronicling the American revolutionary leader's life.
  • Marie Antoinette (2006) and The Affair of the Necklace (2001): Both films delve into the intrigues of French royalty at the close of the century.

Choosing the Right Movie or Show for Your Interests

Each viewer’s interest in the 18th century might differ. Some of us lean towards political and royal dramas, others prefer adventure on the high seas, and some seek detailed biographical accounts. When picking your next watch:

  • Consider whether you prefer cinematic grandeur or episodic depth that TV series offer.
  • Look for content with strong critical acclaim or historical consultation for accuracy.
  • Supplement entertainment with documentaries or historical reads to deepen your understanding.
Tip: To enrich your viewing experience, watch supporting documentaries or visit history websites about the 18th century. This adds context that can make period dramas even more fascinating.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQ)

What defines a movie or show as "18th century" period drama?

An 18th century period drama is set predominantly between 1700 and 1799, focusing on historical events, lifestyles, and settings from that era. These productions emphasize authentic costumes, architecture, and social customs of the time.

Are 18th century movies historically accurate?

While many aim for historical authenticity in costumes and events, filmmakers sometimes take creative liberties for storytelling. It’s best to watch critically and supplement with historical research if accuracy is essential.

Which 18th century movies or shows are best for learning about the period?

Miniseries like John Adams and films like Amadeus or Barry Lyndon offer rich historical insights alongside engaging narratives.

Where can I watch 18th century movies and TV shows?

Many titles are available on streaming platforms like Netflix, Amazon Prime, and Hulu. Some older films might be found on classic movie services or through rental on platforms like iTunes or Google Play.

Can I find 18th century-themed content for children?

Yes, family-friendly titles like Felicity: An American Girl Adventure introduce children to 18th century life in an engaging and age-appropriate way.
