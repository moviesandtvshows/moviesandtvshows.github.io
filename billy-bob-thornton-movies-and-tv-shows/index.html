About Billy Bob Thornton

Billy Bob Thornton (born August 4, 1955) is an American actor, filmmaker, musician, and songwriter. He rose to fame with his Academy Award-winning work in the film Sling Blade (1996), which he wrote, directed, and starred in. Known for his diverse roles in films like A Simple Plan, Monster's Ball, and Bad Santa, Thornton has also gained acclaim in TV series such as Fargo and Goliath. Beyond acting, he is the lead vocalist of the rock band The Boxmasters and is recognized for his unique blend of artistry.

Movies

  • Sling Blade (1996) – Thornton wrote, directed, and starred as Karl Childers, a mentally challenged man returning to his hometown, earning an Academy Award for Best Adapted Screenplay.
  • A Simple Plan (1998) – Delivers a powerful performance in this thriller about greed and moral conflict, earning an Oscar nomination for Best Supporting Actor.
  • Monster's Ball (2001) – Plays a complex role in this acclaimed drama exploring themes of grief and redemption.
  • Bad Santa (2003) – Stars in this dark comedy as a cynical, alcoholic con man posing as a mall Santa.
  • The Man Who Wasn't There (2001) – Features in this Coen Brothers’ noir film as a barber entangled in a web of deceit.

TV Series

  • Fargo (2014) – Stars as Lorne Malvo, a menacing and charismatic antagonist in the critically acclaimed first season.
  • Goliath (2016–2021) – Plays Billy McBride, a washed-up lawyer fighting corruption, earning a Golden Globe Award.
  • Hearts Afire (1992–1995) – Early TV role in this CBS sitcom that helped establish his career.
  • Justified (2013) – Guest role as Robert Quarles, a villainous businessman in this crime drama series.
  • Patriots Day (2016, TV Appearance) – Featured in TV programs covering the dramatization of the Boston Marathon bombing incident.
