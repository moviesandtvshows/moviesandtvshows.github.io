1960s movies and tv shows

A curated list of notable movies and TV shows from the 1960s, spanning various genres and styles that have left a lasting cultural impact.

2001: A Space Odyssey

A visionary sci-fi film exploring humanity's evolution and space exploration directed by Stanley Kubrick (1968).

Adam-12

TV series about the daily lives of two Los Angeles police officers, blending realistic police work with drama (1968–1975).

Alfred Hitchcock Hour

Suspense anthology series hosted by Alfred Hitchcock with thrilling tales of mystery and intrigue (1962–1965).

Batman

A campy and colorful TV series about the adventures of the superhero Batman and his sidekick Robin (1966–1968).

Bewitched

A sitcom about a witch who tries to live a normal suburban life with her mortal husband (1964–1972).

Bonnie and Clyde

A dramatic crime film about the notorious outlaw couple during the Great Depression (1967).

Butch Cassidy and the Sundance Kid

A classic western film that follows two outlaws on the run in the American West (1969).

Cleopatra

Epic historical drama about the life of the Egyptian queen Cleopatra, known for its grand scale and production (1963).

Doctor Who

British sci-fi TV series following the adventures of the time-traveling Doctor (started in 1963, ongoing).

The Dirty Dozen

War film about a group of convicts on a suicide mission during World War II (1967).

The Dick Van Dyke Show

A beloved sitcom about a TV comedy writer balancing work and family life (1961–1966).

The Graduate

Coming-of-age drama featuring a young man caught in a complicated affair after college (1967).

Gilligan's Island

Comedy series about seven castaways stranded on an uncharted island (1964–1967).

Goldfinger

One of the iconic James Bond films featuring the spy's mission to stop the villain Goldfinger (1964).

Hogan's Heroes

A comedic TV series set in a German POW camp during World War II (1965–1971).

How the West Was Won

Epic Western film chronicling the pioneering experiences of a family in the American West (1962/63).

Lawrence of Arabia

Biographical epic about T.E. Lawrence’s exploits during World War I in the Arabian Peninsula (1962).

Midnight Cowboy

Drama about a naive Texan who moves to New York City and forms a friendship with a con man (1969).

Mission: Impossible

Spy drama series featuring an elite covert team carrying out high-risk missions (1966–1973).

My Fair Lady

Musical film adaptation about a professor teaching a flower girl to speak properly (1964).

The Odd Couple

Comedy film about two mismatched roommates with clashing personalities (1968).

Star Trek

Iconic sci-fi TV series chronicling the space adventures of the starship Enterprise crew (1966–1969).

The Sound of Music

Musical film about a governess bringing joy to a widowed naval captain's family in Austria (1965).

The Twilight Zone

Anthology series presenting unusual, eerie, and thought-provoking stories (1959–1964, continuing influence).

Valley of the Dolls

Drama film about three women chasing fame and facing personal struggles in show business (1967).

The Virginian

Western TV series centering on a ranch foreman dealing with life and justice in the Old West (1962–1971).

The Wild Wild West

A blend of western and spy genres featuring secret agents in the Old West (1965–1969).
