1800s movies and tv shows

  • Little Women (2019)
    A faithful adaptation of Louisa May Alcott's novel about the lives, struggles, and aspirations of the four March sisters growing up during and after the American Civil War.

 

  • The Great Train Robbery (1978)
    A heist movie set in the 1800s, where a member of high society secretly leads a band of criminals to rob a moving train carrying gold.

 

  • Amistad (1997)
    This historical drama depicts the 1839 mutiny on the Spanish slave ship La Amistad and the subsequent legal battle in American courts about the freedom of the Africans involved.

 

  • The Good, the Bad, and the Ugly (1966)
    A classic Western set during the American Civil War following three gunslingers competing to find buried Confederate gold.

 

  • The Man Who Would Be King (1975)
    Two ex-soldiers in British India travel to a remote part of Afghanistan in the 1880s to become kings, exploring ambition and colonialism.

 

  • Barry Lyndon (1975)
    Chronicles the rise and fall of an 18th-century Irish adventurer, noted for its portrayal of European aristocracy and life.

 

  • The Elephant Man (1980)
    Tells the story of Joseph Merrick, a severely deformed man in Victorian England, highlighting his intelligence and sensitivity.

 

  • Gone With The Wind (1939)
    An epic tale set during the American Civil War and Reconstruction, touching on themes of love, survival, and societal change in the 19th century South.

 

18th century movies and tv shows

  • The Duchess (2008)
    Based on the life of Georgiana Cavendish, an 18th-century aristocrat, the film explores her unhappy marriage and her popularity with the public, highlighting the challenges faced by women in aristocratic society.

 

  • Dangerous Liaisons (1988)
    Set in the late 18th-century French aristocracy, this drama focuses on a bet between two ex-lovers to seduce an innocent woman, unfolding a story full of scandal, manipulation, and intrigue.

 

  • Rob Roy (1995)
    In 1713 Scotland, Rob Roy MacGregor becomes an outlaw seeking revenge against a nobleman, evading Redcoats, and struggling with charges of treason while defending his family and honor.

 

  • The Death of Louis XIV (2016)
    The film follows the last days of King Louis XIV of France in 1715, depicting his gradual decline amidst the loyal followers surrounding him in the royal chambers.

 

  • Black Sails (2014–2017 TV Series)
    A historical adventure series about Captain Flint and his pirates fighting for survival and treasure on New Providence Island during the early 18th century.

 

1950s movies and tv shows

  • I Love Lucy (1951–1957)
    An iconic American sitcom starring Lucille Ball and Desi Arnaz, following Lucy Ricardo's comedic attempts to break into show business despite having no talent, leading to hilarious predicaments.

 

  • Leaves It to Beaver (1957–1963)
    A classic family sitcom depicting the misadventures and moral lessons of young Theodore “Beaver” Cleaver and his suburban family.

 

  • Father Knows Best (1954–1960)
    A wholesome sitcom focusing on the Anderson family, led by wise father Jim, as they navigate daily life challenges in the 1950s.

 

  • Gunsmoke (1955–1975)
    A long-running Western drama centered on U.S. Marshal Matt Dillon maintaining law and order in frontier Dodge City.

 

  • The Ten Commandments (1956)
    Cecil B. DeMille’s epic retelling of the biblical story of Moses leading the Hebrews out of Egyptian slavery, noted for its grand scale and production.
