Biographical Overview of Anya Taylor-Joy

Anya-Josephine Marie Taylor-Joy (born April 16, 1996) is an American-born Argentine-British actress known for her stunning performances in film and television. Raised between Buenos Aires and London, she left school at 16 to pursue acting full-time. Taylor-Joy gained international acclaim for her lead role in the critically acclaimed horror film "The Witch" (2015) and catapulted to global stardom with her Emmy and Golden Globe-winning performance as Beth Harmon in the Netflix miniseries "The Queen's Gambit" (2020). She continues to star in a range of genres from psychological thrillers like "Split" (2016) to period dramas such as "Emma" (2020) and voices Princess Peach in "The Super Mario Bros. Movie" (2023).
